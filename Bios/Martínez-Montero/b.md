Tania Martínez-Montero holds two masters degree in psychology and clinical neuropsychology, and an MSc in Cognitive and Behavioral Neuroscience from the University of Granada, Spain. She is currently a PhD Candidate at ReState research group project “When Default is not Default”, at the University of Bergen. Her project focuses on vigilance and arousal fluctuations and how those fluctuations affect the reliability of brain networks studies.
